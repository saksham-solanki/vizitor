### Why Contactless Attendance Matters in a Hybrid Model

With hybrid work models on the rise, businesses face new challenges in tracking attendance. Employees come to the office only on specific days, while others work remotely. Manually tracking attendance through traditional means, or even with physical card swipes, becomes a cumbersome and inefficient process. Contactless attendance solutions address these challenges by providing accurate, real-time data on employee attendance, regardless of where they are working from.

According to a Gartner survey, by 2025, over 60% of businesses are expected to implement contactless technologies for attendance and security in hybrid work environments. This shift to contactless systems is not just a trend; it’s a necessity as businesses strive to balance flexibility, safety, and productivity.

By adopting contactless attendance systems, businesses can:

**.** Streamline attendance tracking without requiring employees to physically interact with any devices.

**.** Ensure data accuracy with automation, reducing human errors and ensuring accountability.


**.** Enhance employee safety by minimizing physical touchpoints in the workplace, which is crucial in post-pandemic environments.

**.** Provide real-time insights into employee attendance trends, making it easier to manage hybrid teams effectively.

Vizitor's contactless attendance management solution addresses all of these needs, helping businesses optimize their attendance tracking and workforce management in the hybrid world.

### The Role of Technology in Shaping Contactless Attendance

The adoption of smart technologies has been a game changer in the world of attendance management, especially in hybrid work settings. Key technologies driving the future of contactless attendance include facial recognition, mobile app-based check-ins, and geofencing.

#### Facial Recognition

Facial recognition is one of the most effective and secure methods for contactless attendance. Employees no longer need to use passwords or remember any credentials. Instead, they can simply walk up to a sensor, and the system will automatically recognize them and mark their attendance.

Vizitor’s facial recognition system is highly accurate and quick, offering a secure, touch-free way to log employee attendance. The technology works seamlessly and ensures that employees can check in with ease, even in a busy office environment.

According to a MarketsandMarkets report, the global facial recognition market is expected to grow from $3.8 billion in 2020 to $8.5 billion by 2025, driven by the widespread adoption of contactless technologies in both security and operational functions.

#### Mobile App-Based Check-Ins

In a hybrid model, employees might not always be in the office, so the ability to track their attendance remotely is crucial. Vizitor’s mobile app allows employees to check in using their smartphones. The app uses GPS, Wi-Fi, or QR codes to verify the employee's location, ensuring that attendance is tracked accurately, whether the employee is at the office or working from home.

This flexibility makes it easy for companies to track attendance across different locations without needing to install specialized hardware at every remote site.

#### Geofencing

Geofencing technology creates a virtual boundary around a workplace or designated work zone. When an employee enters or exits the predefined area, their attendance is automatically logged. This is particularly useful for companies with hybrid teams or those in industries like construction or field services.

Vizitor’s geofencing feature makes it easy for businesses to manage their hybrid workforce. It can detect when employees arrive at or leave a specific location and logs attendance based on those movements, helping businesses keep accurate records without any manual input.

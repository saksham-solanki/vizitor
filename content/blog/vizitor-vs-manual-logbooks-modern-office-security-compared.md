## The Old Way: Manual Logbooks

For decades, visitor logbooks were the default tool for front-desk recordkeeping. Visitors would jot down their name, purpose, and contact details and that was it.\
It’s simple, yes. But simplicity often comes with trade-offs.

### **Common Issues with Manual Logbooks**

1. **Illegible Entries** - Handwritten data is often hard to read or incomplete.
2. **Poor Data Security** - Anyone can flip through pages and view previous visitors’ details.
3. **No Verification** - No way to confirm the identity or authenticity of the visitor.
4. **Compliance Gaps** - Paper records can’t meet GDPR or ISO security standards.
5. **No Analytics** - You can’t track visitor frequency, duration, or patterns.
6. **Inefficient Audits** - Searching past entries can take hours, not seconds.

In short, logbooks might record visitors but they don’t **protect** your workplace.

## The New Standard: Digital Visitor Management Systems

A **Visitor Management System (VMS)** automates and secures every stage of the visitor journey from check-in to exit using technology.

### How It Works

* Visitors scan a **QR code or use a tablet** at the reception to check in.
* The host receives an **instant notification** (SMS, email, or Slack).
* A **visitor badge** is generated with photo and access permissions.
* All data is securely stored in the cloud, with audit trails and reports.

It’s faster, more secure, and fully compliant with modern data regulations.

What could be a better way to thrive at work? There seems to be something special about coworking spaces. According to the [researchers ](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/314750463_How_Does_Thriving_Matter_at_Workplace), there are multiple factors that enhance the nature of thriving in the workplace. The studies were surprising that people who belong to them report levels of thriving that approach an average of 6 on a 7-point scale. These numbers are higher than  the average for employees who do their jobs in regular offices.

Recently, more companies have started to model their office spaces to look more like those of coworking spaces as these modern office structures yield higher productivity and rates of satisfaction. Different types of professionals have confirmed that coworking spaces helps them to grow professionally.

**There are some reasons that help coworkers flourish **
A coworking is simply defined as comfortable working environment shared between a community of independent professionals and small businesses. These places are quickly becoming the most desirable way to work, but they aren’t just an update to modern furniture and shared office space. 

**People find the coworking spaces quite productive place to work**
Your work environment has a direct impact on your productivity levels, Apart from the work they do, the people love this space because they are able to bring their complete selves to the workplace. 
The coworking environment comprises of a group of individuals working for different projects, organisations and ventures, unlike normal offices.

- The coworking spaces face very minimal competition and little to no in-house politics, so that coworkers  do not feel as though they need to put on a particular persona for them to fit into that environment.

- Secondly, people who work in a shared office space are more likely to help one another out when faced with a problem. The culture in these environments is to try and uplift each other.

**Coworkers feel as a part of community**

One of the main reasons to rent out a coworking space instead of an office space in the first place is because of the community. Each coworking space has its own unique vibe and group of people. The people in charge of managing each coworking space will often go the extra mile to ensure that they have created an experience that is memorable, and which can easily meet the professional requirements of all the people who will be using that space.

## **Wrapping up**

Coworking spaces are here to stay. The many benefits offered by these spaces means that they will only become more popular. People who work out of coworking spaces tend to thrive because of the sense of community of coworking spaces, the flexibility, the potential for innovation, learning and collaboration, the opportunity to build strong personal and professional relationships, and the spaces’ focus on great design and wellbeing.

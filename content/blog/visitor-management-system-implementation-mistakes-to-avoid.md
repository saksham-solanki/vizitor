## MISTAKE 1: Failing to consider user experience

Failing to consider user experience when implementing a visitor management system can be a serious mistake.

• The system should be designed to be easy and intuitive to use, both for employees who are responsible for managing visitors and for visitors themselves.

• The system's user interface must be easy to use for visitors and employees. A poorly designed or implemented system can frustrate users, cause delays, and reduce the overall effectiveness of the system.


{{< figure src="/images/blog/bigstock-Ux-User-Experience-User-Inte-274189435.png" >}}


• If the system is difficult to navigate or understand, it could lead to frustration and inefficient use, and could even discourage visitors from returning. Moreover, a poorly designed system could negatively impact the reputation of an organization.
 

• To avoid this mistake, it's essential to involve employees and visitors in the system's design process. Ensuring that visitors have a seamless sign-in experience in a modern and visually impactful way can help project a professional image. 

• Organizations must think about the bigger picture when it comes to visitor management solutions, including addressing potential user experience barriers, providing ease-of-use, and user-centered design.

## MISTAKE 2: Failing to provide adequate training

If employees are not trained on how to use the system correctly, it can lead to errors, reduced efficiency, confusion or delay in the check-in process, which can negatively impact the visitor experience. 
It's important to provide clear instructions on how to use the system and to ensure that all users are comfortable with the process.

{{< figure src="/images/blog/trainingfailsmain.jpg" >}}

• To avoid this mistake, it is essential to provide thorough training to employees. This can involve training sessions, online resources, and user manuals to help employees understand how the system operates and how to utilize its full capabilities.

• During the training phase, employees should be shown how to handle different visitor scenarios, such as pre-booked meeting visitors or employees who require access permissions. 

• By prioritizing comprehensive training, organizations can ensure that their employees are capable of using the visitor management system successfully. This, in turn, leads to a more efficient and productive workplace, faster check-ins, reduced errors, and a better visitor experience.

## MISTAKE 3: Not defining clear goals

Every organization has unique needs and requirements for their visitor management system, and these needs should guide the design and selection of the system.

• Without clear goals, an organization may end up with a system that does not address their specific security and compliance requirements, leading to ineffective and inefficient use of resources.

• Organizations must have a clear understanding of what they expect to gain from implementing a [**Visitor management systems**](/best-visitor-management-system-in-india). This will help ensure that the system is fit for purpose and delivers the desired results.

{{< figure src="/images/blog/systems-vs-goals-700x467.jpg" >}}

• To avoid this mistake, an organization should establish clear goals such as managing visitor data, scheduling appointments or meetings, pre-registering visitors, and ensuring compliance with data protection regulations such as GDPR. 



• Goals should be specific, measurable and achievable to help the organization identify the appropriate system and evaluate its effectiveness. Proper goal setting helps identify gaps in service delivery, reduces errors, and helps ensure timely delivery of services.

## MISTAKE 4: Ineffective communication

Employee and visitor awareness of the new system is vital. 

• Organizations should educate employees about the system's functioning and inform visitors about the necessary requirements to sign-in, including data privacy policies and how to use the system to save time and foster credibility.

• Otherwise, it can cause confusion, delays, and even security breaches, especially when visitors and employees don't understand the check-in and check-out procedures.

{{< figure src="/images/blog/ineffective_communications.png" >}}

• To avoid this mistake, organizations should communicate clearly with both visitors and employees about the new system. They should provide appropriate training and clear instructions on how to use the system, including any specific data protection policies or instructions on how to pre-book a visit. 

• Additionally, employees should be provided with detailed information on when to use the system and how to handle different visitor scenarios.



• Providing information in advance through pre-registration or during the confirmation process can also help minimize confusion and speed up the sign-in process. A real-time digital sign-in process provides an exciting welcome to visitors, particularly coupled with digital touchless technology that helps to reinforce a positive image of the organization and provide a memorable experience for the visitor

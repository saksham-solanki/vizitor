Nearly every business organization receives a diverse range of visitors and guests each day.They may be your regular guests, visitors, suppliers, clients or even contractors.

There is a great need to manage all of them in a consistent and clear manner.Some visitors return on a regular basis, while others come once and never return. Regardless of the type of business you run, there are certain security concerns that must be addressed in relation to visitors.


There should be a well-planned policy to manage visitors and guests to create a hassle free environment. There may be certain challenges faced by the reception staff while encountering visitors like some visitors may leave waiting in lobby areas for hours, some visitors may enter without approval and much more.

These only are the results of not having a set and strong visitor policy at workplaces.

“A visitor policy is essential for ensuring the safety of visitors. This policy ensures the safety of your employees, the security of your equipment, and the confidentiality of your confidential information.”

## Some tips and suggestions for a great visitor policy are-

### 1. Restriction on office access 

In terms of security, the visitor policy and procedure should include information about authorized access. It should be stated which areas of the office are accessible to which types of visitors. We are aware that there are some confidential information rooms in our workplace. So there should be a confidential visitor sign in the book to secure those rooms. As a result, these areas can be restricted from outside visitors.

Define which areas of the office visitors are allowed to access. Sensitive areas such as server rooms or executive offices should have restricted access. By using a clear policy, you ensure that visitors only enter areas where they are authorized.

### 2. Keep Visitors' record 

It is highly important to track who has visited your premises and keep a record of them for future purposes and emergencies.One of the best ways to do this is to implement a digital logbook based on cloud server storage.

It eliminates confidentiality risks and makes records easily accessible for future reference. This step is one of the major point to be added in your visitor policy.

Tracking who visits your office is essential for both security and operational efficiency. A digital logbook stored on a cloud server ensures that visitor information is secure and easily accessible for future reference. This record is invaluable in case of security issues or emergencies.


### 3. Identify your Visitors 

There should be a  proper and strong identification procedure to identify your visitors and guests to allow only authorized access to your workplaces. One of the best ways is to implement a Visitor Badge System to issue authenticated visitor badges to visitors to enhance the check-in process. E-badges contain information such as the visitor’s name, host name, and the reason for their visit to the workplace. This method is a low-cost and relatively simple way to promote easy recognition of guests.Visitors office system also provides the visitor badge templates to the company.

Implement an effective identification process for visitors, such as issuing visitor badges. These badges should contain vital information such as the visitor's name, the host’s name, and the reason for their visit. This allows staff to quickly identify authorized visitors and prevents unauthorized access.


### 4. Visitor policies for recording 

Almost all  businesses are concerned about the security of visitor data. As a result, they may impose specific restrictions on visitors. Details such as appropriate guest use of your wi-fi network and technology, as well as any restrictions on photography and/or recording equipment, should be specified in your visitor policy. If certain guests are required to sign non-disclosure agreements, that is another important detail to document.

Ensure that your policy covers all aspects of visitor behavior, including the use of office Wi-Fi, restrictions on photography or recording, and the need for visitors to sign Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) where applicable. Clearly communicating these policies ensures that visitors are aware of their responsibilities.



### 5. Escort your guests while they are at premises 

Escort your guests and guests with a friendly and warm welcome to their hosts when they enter your  premises.They should be welcomed as long as they are in your premises rather than leaving them alone to wander in the office. Notify your hosts of their guests arrivals so that they could make their specific arrangements for meetings.

For security, visitors should be escorted at all times while in restricted or sensitive areas. This practice not only ensures their safety but also provides a positive visitor experience. Notify the appropriate host upon a visitor’s arrival so that arrangements can be made for meetings.

### 6. Establish  strategy for enforcing and educating 

To eliminate confusion and keep your office safe, you must establish clear guidelines. Employees should know what to do if they see an unaccompanied visitor in a restricted area. Establish your evacuation plans in advance to reduce the risks.

You must educate your employees on the visitor policy. When you create a guest policy, make sure your entire staff is aware of it. This way, your staff will prevent visitors from entering the confidential area.Training and drills will ensure that your employees understand and trust the security policies in place.

Make sure your staff understands the visitor policy and knows how to handle unaccompanied visitors in restricted areas. Regular training and drills will ensure that everyone is well-prepared to enforce the policy and maintain security.



### 7. Implement a convenient  VMS to control visitor flows 

Controlling, managing and tracking visitors is extremely crucial for an organization.Visitor Management system offers a more efficient and full-proof sign-in process. This is the secure way to manage your visitors. With this system you can monitor the visitors and you can know who is in the building at any day/time and keep an accurate record of them.

Various key features like Touchless check-ins, pre-registrations, NDAs etc. make your office more secure and safe.

A VMS can automate many aspects of the visitor process, such as check-ins, badge printing, and tracking. Features like touchless check-ins and real-time visitor tracking improve security and save time. With a VMS, you can always know who is in the building and when, making it an essential tool for managing visitor flows.


### 8. Improve your company’s bottom line 

Using the NDA policy, this security practice allows the organization to clearly state what is expected of the visitor while on it’s property. This statement could include everything from protecting trade secrets within the facility to declaring to what extent the organization is liable for any injury that may occur. 

Clearly outline expectations for visitors during their time on your property. An NDA can be a critical component in protecting your company’s trade secrets, intellectual property, and sensitive information. It can also clarify the company's liability in case of accidents.


### 9. Create a fantastic visiting experience for guests 

One of the main aspects of your office policy is to make your guests feel welcomed in a friendly and warm way.Make it your one of the most important goals to make visitors feel welcome when they step into your reception area instead of making them feel uncomfortable and embarrassed. Make your reception and lobby area congestion-free and peaceful.

A positive visitor experience begins at the reception. Greet visitors warmly and ensure that your reception area is organized and free from congestion. This will help set the tone for a professional, welcoming environment that reflects well on your business.



### 10. Review regularly for updation 

Review your visitor office policy regularly to make necessary updates and changes Keep it up-to-date as per your changing needs and latest technological innovations.Take feedback from your customers,employees and guests to adjust your visitor policy as per their considerations.

A visitor policy is not a static document. As your company grows and technology evolves, it's essential to periodically review and update your policy to reflect changing needs and new security threats. Gathering feedback from employees and visitors can help refine the process and ensure that it remains effective.

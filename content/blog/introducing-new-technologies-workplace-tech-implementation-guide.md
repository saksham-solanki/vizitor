Introducing a  new technology in the workplace can be challenging as people often don't like change, especially when it comes to changing their existing work scenarios. Making a change can be a tough task when you want to implement a new tool and sometimes may not go with your existing system. 

Also,there may arise a situation where some of your employees and leaders will not get ready to opt for the new transition and often make it challenging for business owners to convince everyone of the [benefits of the new technology](https://medium.com/akeo-tech/5-reasons-why-adopting-new-technology-is-important-for-any-business-b0ac113d4019).

After all,implementing digital and innovative technologies at the workplaces can enhance productivity and efficiency of the businesses and help to stay ahead of the market competition.
Adopting innovative technology opens the doors of exciting benefits and opportunities for businesses.
But, 
We all know that there might be some challenges along the journey of improving the existing system like setting up a new tool and making it work smoothly with what you already have. 

**Common challenges faced by business owners in implementing a new technology–**


• Not able to adjust new technology with the existing system 

• Failing to win staff over to the new technology.

• Not implementing the right procedure to use it

• Not providing proper training to staff to utilize the product 

• Fail to make the team aware of the benefits the tool offers 

So, to overcome the difficulties of the process, you just need to explore some simple tips and strategies given ahead in the article to guide you through the process of introducing a new technology and to make your team aware of the value of the new tool.


## Tips and Strategies for introducing a new technology at Workplace–

###  Build a Strong Roll-out Plan

Did you know that 70% of implementations of new tools fail due to poor planning?
To successfully implement a new tool in the workplace, it is necessary to develop a well-thought plan that includes timelines of steps involved, basic tasks and instructions, information about organizational benefits, involved stakeholders etc.
Your plan may vary depending on the type of tool you are looking to implement, but it must include some key elements such as–

**Specific Action Dates-** Include specific dates when your technology is ready for roll-out, when you will let your employees make a switch etc. Before scheduling the roll out plan, it is crucial to track the status of development of the new tool,its stability and reliability. 

**Deployment tiers-** Include specific strategy into the plan to deploy your tool successfully among various departments at your workplace. 

**Training sessions-** Include information regarding what type of training you will need to make your employees successfully utilize the technology. Schedule different training sessions for different employees of individual departments. 

The goal of your roll out plan must be to provide clear and easily understandable information about the technology. Keep it Simple and flexible and listen to your employee’s feedback regularly to improve your roll-out plan for introducing tools in future.

###  Communicate Effectively with the team 

To overcome the difficulties of adapting the new technology by the team, effectively communicate the benefits of the new technology to your teams.

Understand the reasons for resistance to new technology by employees and help them to adapt to new technologies. Talk about how it can make their work easier, faster, or more efficient.Make your staff open to ask any concerns or questions they may have and be patient in answering them.Organise some training sessions to share examples and stories to help them [understand the practical applications of the technology](https://elearningindustry.com/how-to-train-employees-on-a-new-system).
According to Gartner, it can take around 18-24 months for organizations to fully adopt and integrate a new technology into their workflows.

Also, make sure everyone knows the goals and purpose of your new technology. Make your team members aware of the personal value of the tool and how it will impact their day-to-day work. 

###  Create a pilot program

Before implementing the technology on full scale, create a small pilot program to test the new tool with a small group of team members. This helps you to address any issues with the new technology before implementing it company wide.

Select a group of team members to test out the new tool with a specific timeline. Make them clear about the goals and benefits of the tool and gather feedback from them regularly about their experience of using the tool. 

Also, communicate with the pilot group to identify any challenges and issues they face using the technology and try to fix them to improve the technology for future.

###  Provide Engaging Training 

When implementing a  new technology,making the employees understand how to use it can be a challenge for the owners. It may take time for everyone to fully adopt and become comfortable with the new technology.

To make everyone ready to opt for the new technology, provide engaging training sessions to share the benefits the technology brings to their work.

Some companies prefer to hire an external expert technology provider to help the team about the benefits and services of the technology. Organize online workshops, seminars and online tutorials to familiarize members with the benefits and features of the tool. 

Share some [success stories](https://forbytes.com/blog/top-most-impressive-cases-when-technology-helped-businesses/) to motivate and inspire your employees to make a switch from the existing system. Encourage employees to ask questions and provide ongoing support as they learn to use the tool.
A study found that 40% of employees prefer just-in-time training (i.e., learning when they need it) as highly valuable.

**💡Tips for effective training–**

• No one wants to sit through a boring training session.Make your training sessions enjoyable by introducing some fun activities as more employees will like to join id training is enjoyable.

• Don't let your employees sit for long in one go. Organize your sessions in small parts to engage more members.

• Provide them with some online tutorials, guides videos and resources to help them learn of their own when they want.

• To help everyone learn effectively, offer a variety of materials and choices during training sessions that suit each person's way of learning.



###  Evaluate it Regularly 

Another important step to implement the new technology successfully is to evaluate the performance of the technology after installation. It is crucial to track whether the users are satisfied with the performance of the system or not. Also, it is always better to keep an eye on issues and challenges in advance to address and resolve them as they arise. 

Track progress of your tool weekly generating evacuation reports and data insights on the usage of the tool.
If you are still not able to reach your goal, then make more efforts and hold up more training sessions and workshops to get it done by the end. Regularly review the feedback from the users and use it to identify the areas where improvement is needed.

As we know technology is evolving on days off and it is important to make continuous improvements and updates to enhance the capabilities of the tool to stay ahead in the marketplace.


###  Celebrate your Success

It is not important to celebrate only milestones and achievements. Celebrate your success, no matter how small it is. Celebrate your small wins with the team and it’s also important to reward employees for their contributions.
Share your success stories and experiences resulting from implementing a new system. This helps to create a more positive and enthusiastic atmosphere within the workplace.
